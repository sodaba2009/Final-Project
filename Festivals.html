Afghan Festivals:

Nawruz_ The Afghan New Year, is celebrate on the first day of spring (march 21st) celebrated with family gatherings, traditional foods, musics, and dancing. People clean their homes, wear new clothes, and vivit family and friends. Special dishes "Sheer Kormas" (a sweat milk-based dish) and "Samarqand Pilaf" are prepared for the occasion.

Eid al-Fitr_ Eid comes at the end of Ramadan, the Islamic holy month of fasting. It is a time for celebration , with families coming together to enjoy special meals and share giftd. The day starts with prayers at the mosque, followed by visiting the relatives and neighbors. Sweets like "Baklava" and "Sheer korma" are commonly served, and itis also a time for giving to charity.

Eid al-Adha_ Another important Islamic festival, celebrated to honor the willingness of prophet Ibrahim to sacrifice his son in obedience to God. On this day, Muslims in Afghanistan, as in manyparts of the world, perform the ritual of Qurbani (animal sacrifice). The meat is shared among family, friends, and the needy. Special prayers are said, and the festival is an importanttime for charity and community spirit.
